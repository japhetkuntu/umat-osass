## 1. Overview

The **OSASS Non-Academic Promotion Portal** is the dedicated web application for UMaT non-academic staff to apply for career promotion. It mirrors the Academic Portal in structure but is tailored to the specific scoring criteria and career path of non-academic roles — including administrative, technical, and support staff categories.

**Key capabilities:**
- Check promotion eligibility in real time
- Plan your career progression with the Promotion Forecast tool
- Build and submit a promotion application across three scored categories: Performance at Work, Knowledge & Profession, and Service
- Track the review progress through institutional review committees
- Download your official promotion letter upon approval

## 2. Getting Started

### 2.1 Logging In

![Login Page](screenshots/01-login.png)

**Steps to log in:**

1. Navigate to the Non-Academic Portal URL. The **Sign In** page loads immediately.
2. Enter your **Staff ID or Email** (e.g., `a.boateng@umat.edu.gh`).
3. Enter your **Password**.
4. Click the **Sign In** button.

On a successful login, you will be redirected to the **Dashboard**.

> **Note:** If you have not received your credentials, contact the Human Resources Office or IT Support at `support@umat.edu.gh`.

---

### 2.2 Forgot Password

![Forgot Password](screenshots/02-forgot-password.png)

1. Click **"Forgot password?"** on the login page.
2. Enter your registered **Email Address** and click **Send OTP**.
3. Check your email for the one-time code.
4. Use the OTP and set a new password when prompted.
5. Click **Back to sign in** once complete.

## 4. Eligibility Check

Before starting an application, verify that you meet all promotion criteria on the **Promotion Eligibility Assessment** page.

![Eligibility](screenshots/04-eligibility.png)

**How to access:** Click **Eligibility** in the left sidebar.

**What the page shows:**

| Section | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Overall status banner** | Green (Eligible) or Amber (Action Required) with a summary message |
| **Years in Current Rank** | Progress bar showing years served vs. minimum required (e.g., *6.2 / 5* means 6.2 years against a 5-year minimum — requirement **met**) |
| **Performance Requirements** | Submitted performance levels across Performance at Work, Knowledge, and Service; indicates whether the required performance combination has been achieved |
| **Knowledge Requirements** | Whether minimum qualifications or certificates have been submitted |

**What to do:**
- If **Eligible**: Proceed to start or continue your application.
- If **Not Eligible**: Review which specific criteria are unmet and update the relevant sections of your application profile.

## 5. Promotion Forecast

The **Promotion Forecast** tool shows your complete non-academic career promotion path and helps you plan for future milestones.

![Forecast](screenshots/05-forecast.png)

**How to access:** Click **Promotion Forecast** in the sidebar.

**What the page shows:**

- Your current position and date of last promotion
- For each career milestone:
  - Target position and eligibility status
  - Years required vs. years served
  - Estimated eligibility date for future milestones
  - A prioritised list of **required actions** (e.g., *"Document your service activities"*, *"Obtain a professional certificate"*)
  - Any performance gap analysis (current vs. required scores)
- Future milestones are shown as **locked** until intermediate promotions are completed

## 6. Promotion Application

The non-academic promotion application consists of three scored sections. All sections must be completed before the application can be submitted.

### 6.1 Application Overview

![Application Overview](screenshots/06-application-overview.png)

**How to access:** Click **Promotion App** in the sidebar or **"Continue Application"** on the dashboard.

The overview displays three category cards:

| Category | Max Score | Description |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Performance at Work** | 60 | Ratings from supervisors on work quality, efficiency, attendance, and attitude |
| **Knowledge & Profession** | 30 | Academic qualifications, professional certifications, and training completions |
| **Service** | 20 | Institutional service roles performed within UMaT |

Click any card to enter that section.

---

### 6.2 Performance at Work Section

![Performance Section](screenshots/07-performance-section.png)

**How to access:** Click the **Performance at Work** card or navigate via **Promotion App → Performance at Work**.

**What to do in this section:**
1. **Add performance ratings** for each assessment year within the promotion period:
   - Select the **category** (e.g., Efficiency, Punctuality, Teamwork)
   - Select the **rating** (Excellent, Good, Satisfactory, Poor)
   - Enter the **year** and **name of rater** (supervisor/HOD)
2. **Upload the performance appraisal document** (official annual appraisal forms signed by your supervisor).
3. Click **Save** after each entry.
4. Click **Mark as Complete** when all ratings are entered.

The system aggregates performance ratings into a numeric score based on the institutional scoring matrix.

---

### 6.3 Knowledge & Profession Section

![Knowledge Section](screenshots/08-knowledge-section.png)

**How to access:** Click the **Knowledge & Profession** card or navigate via **Promotion App → Knowledge & Profession**.

**What to do in this section:**
1. **Add qualifications and certifications** obtained during the assessment period:
   - Select the **type** from the dropdown (e.g., *Master's Degree*, *Professional Certificate*, *Training Course*)
   - Enter the **name of the qualification/certificate**
   - Enter the **awarding institution** and **year awarded**
2. **Upload supporting documents** (degree certificates, transcripts, course completion letters).
3. Click **Save** for each entry.

Each knowledge material type carries a score defined by the administration. For example, a Master's Degree may carry 10 points while a professional certificate carries 5 points.

---

### 6.4 Service Section

![Service Section](screenshots/09-service-section.png)

**How to access:** Click the **Service** card or navigate via **Promotion App → Service**.

**What to do in this section:**
1. **Add service roles** performed at UMaT:
   - Select the **position type** (e.g., *Head of Unit*, *Secretary to Committee*, *Sports Coordinator*)
   - Enter the **period** of service (start and end dates)
   - Add any supporting **remarks**
2. **Upload a service document** if available.
3. Click **Save** and **Mark as Complete** when all roles are entered.

---

### 6.5 Review & Submit

![Review Page](screenshots/10-review.png)

**How to access:** Navigate to the **Review** page once all three sections show **Completed** status.

**What the Review page shows:**
- A summary of all three sections with scores and completion status
- The overall score vs. the passing threshold
- A checklist verifying all required documents are uploaded

**Submitting:**
1. Review all information carefully — the application **cannot be edited after submission**.
2. Click **Submit Application** and confirm in the dialog.
3. You will receive a confirmation and be redirected to the Progress page.

## 7. Application Progress Tracking

After submission, track your application through the institutional review pipeline.

**How to access:** Click **"View Application Status"** on the dashboard or navigate to `/progress`.

The progress page shows each review stage:

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Submitted** | Application received |
| **Under Review (Institutional Committee)** | Committee is reviewing |
| **Under Review (University Committee)** | University-level review in progress |
| **Approved / Rejected / Returned** | Final decision |

If **Returned**, the committee's notes for correction will be shown and you can resubmit after making the required changes.
