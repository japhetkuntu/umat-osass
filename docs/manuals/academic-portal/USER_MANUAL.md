## 1. Overview

The **OSASS Academic Promotion Portal** is a dedicated web application for UMaT academic staff to manage their entire promotion journey from eligibility verification through application submission to tracking the review outcome. The portal streamlines the academic promotion process by providing a single digital platform that replaces paper-based workflows.

**Key capabilities:**
- Check promotion eligibility in real time
- Plan a career path with the Promotion Forecast tool
- Build and submit a promotion application with supporting documents across three scored categories: Teaching, Publications, and Service
- Track the review progress of a submitted application
- Download your official promotion letter upon approval
- Access official guidelines, score rubrics, and staff announcements

## 2. Getting Started

### 2.1 Landing Page

When you first visit the portal URL, you are greeted by the **OSASS landing page**. This is the public-facing homepage that introduces the system before you log in.

![Landing Page](screenshots/01-landing-page.png)

**What you see:**
- The **UMaT OSASS** logo and tagline *"PROMOTION & APPOINTMENT"*
- The headline *"Every UMaT staff promotion journey, one place."*
- A description of OSASS as the Online Staff Appointment & Promotion System
- A hamburger menu in the top-right corner to access sign-in and information links

**Next step:** Click the menu icon or navigate to `/login` to access the sign-in page.

---

### 2.2 Logging In

![Login Page](screenshots/02-login-page.png)

**Steps to log in:**

1. Navigate to the portal URL. You will be directed to the **Sign In** page.
2. Enter your **Staff ID or Email** (e.g., `kmensah@umat.edu.gh`).
3. Enter your **Password**.
4. Click the **Sign In** button.

On a successful login, you will be redirected to the **Dashboard**.

> **Note:** If you have not yet received your login credentials, contact the Academic Promotion Office or the IT Support desk at `support@umat.edu.gh`.

---

### 2.3 Forgot Password

![Forgot Password](screenshots/03-forgot-password.png)

If you cannot remember your password:

1. Click **"Forgot password?"** on the login page.
2. On the **Reset Your Password** screen, enter your registered **Email Address**.
3. Click **Send OTP**.
4. Check your email inbox for a one-time code (OTP).
5. Enter the OTP and your new password when prompted.
6. Click **Back to sign in** once the password has been reset.

## 4. Eligibility Check

Before starting an application, always verify your eligibility status on the **Promotion Eligibility Assessment** page.

![Eligibility](screenshots/05-eligibility.png)

**How to access:** Click **Eligibility** in the left sidebar.

**What the page shows:**

| Section | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Overall status banner** | Green (Eligible) or Amber (Action Required) banner with a summary message |
| **Years in Current Rank** | Progress bar showing years served vs. the minimum required. For example, *5.3 / 4* means 5.3 years served against a 4-year minimum — requirement **met** |
| **Performance Requirements** | Shows the submitted performance levels for Teaching, Publications, and Service. Indicates whether the required combination (e.g., *Two High levels + one Good level*) has been achieved |
| **Publication Requirements** | Displays your total publications count and the minimum refereed journal requirement |

**Eligibility actions:**
- If **Eligible**: A **"Continue Application"** button will appear on the dashboard and a link to go directly to the application.
- If **Not Eligible**: Review the specific criteria not yet met. The "Complete Your Profile First" button guides you to submit your data before eligibility can be confirmed.

> **Tip:** Navigate to the **Eligibility Guidance** page (accessible from the eligibility results) for detailed explanations of each requirement and tips on how to meet them.

## 5. Promotion Forecast

The **Promotion Forecast** tool gives you a forward-looking view of your entire career promotion path — from your current rank all the way to the highest academic position.

![Forecast](screenshots/06-forecast.png)

**How to access:** Click **Promotion Forecast** in the left sidebar.

**What the page shows:**

- Your **current position** and date of last promotion
- For each milestone on your promotion ladder:
  - **Target position** and whether you are currently eligible to apply
  - Minimum years required vs. years served (e.g., 4 years required, 5.3 years served → **Eligible**)
  - Estimated eligibility date for future milestones (if currently locked)
  - **Publication requirements**: minimum total publications and refereed journal counts
  - **Required actions**: a prioritised checklist of steps to complete (e.g., *"Add 1 more refereed journal article"*, *"Document all service roles"*)
  - A visual timeline showing each promotion step

**Locked milestones:** Future positions are shown as locked if you must first be promoted to an intermediate position. They display the estimated year you will become eligible.

## 6. Promotion Application

The promotion application is divided into three scored sections. You can work on them in any order and save progress at any time.

### 6.1 Application Overview

![Application Overview](screenshots/07-application-overview.png)

**How to access:** Click **Promotion App** in the sidebar, or click **"Continue Application"** on the dashboard.

The overview page shows the three application categories as cards:

| Category | Max Score | Description |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Teaching** | 70 | Undergraduate & postgraduate teaching load, supervision, and course documentation |
| **Publications** | 40 | Journal articles, conference papers, book chapters, and other publication types |
| **Service** | 30 | Administrative service roles held within UMaT |

Each card shows the current status (Not Started / In Progress / Completed) and the score achieved so far. Click any category card to open that section.

---

### 6.2 Teaching Section

![Teaching Section](screenshots/08-teaching-section.png)

**How to access:** Click the **Teaching** card on the application overview, or navigate via **Promotion App → Teaching** in the sidebar.

**What to do in this section:**
1. **Add courses** taught during the assessment period. For each course, enter:
   - Course code and name
   - Level (100–800)
   - Credit hours
   - Semester and academic year
2. **Upload the teaching schedule document** (required) — typically your official teaching load report or timetable.
3. Click **Save** after each entry.
4. When all courses are entered, click **Mark as Complete**.

The system calculates your Teaching score automatically based on total contact hours and the number of courses.

---

### 6.3 Publications Section

![Publications Section](screenshots/09-publications-section.png)

**How to access:** Click the **Publications** card or navigate via **Promotion App → Publications**.

**What to do in this section:**
1. **Add each publication**. For each item, provide:
   - Title of publication
   - Authors (your name must be included)
   - Journal / Conference name
   - Year of publication
   - Publication type (selected from the configured indicator list, e.g., *Scopus-Indexed Journal Article*, *Conference Proceedings*)
2. **Upload the publications document** — a single PDF compiling evidence of all listed publications.
3. Click **Save** after each entry.

Each publication type carries a pre-defined score (set by the Academic Board in the Admin Portal). The section score is the sum of all publication scores.

---

### 6.4 Service Section

![Service Section](screenshots/10-service-section.png)

**How to access:** Click the **Service** card or navigate via **Promotion App → Service**.

**What to do in this section:**
1. **Add service positions** held during the assessment period. For each:
   - Select the position type from the dropdown (e.g., *Head of Department*, *Senate Member*)
   - Enter the period (start and end dates)
   - Add any remarks
2. **Upload the service document** if required.
3. Click **Save** and then **Mark as Complete** when done.

---

### 6.5 Review & Submit

![Review Page](screenshots/11-review.png)

**How to access:** Once all three sections show a status of **Completed** or after filling in your entries, navigate to the **Review** page from the sidebar or the application overview.

**What the Review page shows:**
- A consolidated summary of all three sections — scores, completion status, and entry counts
- An overall score tally with the pass/fail threshold clearly indicated
- A checklist confirming all required documents have been uploaded

**Submitting your application:**
1. Review all information carefully. You **cannot edit** the application after submission.
2. If satisfied, click **Submit Application**.
3. Confirm the submission in the confirmation dialog.
4. You will be redirected to the **Confirmation** page upon successful submission.

> **Important:** Ensure all three sections are complete and all required documents are uploaded before submitting. Missing documents will prevent submission.

---

### 6.6 View Submitted Application

![Application View](screenshots/12-application-view.png)

After submission, you can view a **read-only preview** of your submitted application at any time.

**How to access:** Navigate to **Promotion App → View Application** or click the "View Submitted Application" link on the progress page.

This page shows a structured summary of all the data you submitted, including scores per section, document upload status, and submission date and time.

## 7. Application Progress Tracking

![Application Progress](screenshots/15-application-progress.png)

**How to access:** Click the **"View Application Status"** button on the dashboard (visible only after submission), or navigate to `/progress`.

The **Application Progress** page shows the full review pipeline your submitted application passes through:

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Submitted** | Application received by the system |
| **Under Review (DAPC)** | Department Academic Promotions Committee is reviewing |
| **Under Review (FAPC)** | Faculty Academic Promotions Committee is reviewing |
| **Under Review (UAPC)** | University Academic Promotions Committee is reviewing |
| **Approved / Rejected / Returned** | Final decision made |

A visual timeline highlights the current stage. If the application is **Returned** for corrections, notes from the committee will be displayed and you will be able to resubmit.
